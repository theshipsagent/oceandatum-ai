Pipeline Proximity

Regional Infrastructure Investment

Southeast Louisiana is experiencing significant natural gas infrastructure investment, with $20–30 billion in pipeline, LNG, storage, and power generation projects under construction or planned through 2030. The region's pipeline network connects Haynesville production to Gulf Coast export markets and serves growing industrial and power generation demand across Louisiana and the Gulf Coast.

Part 01

Executive Summary

10+ Natural Gas Systems

Transco, TETCO, Tennessee, Gulf South, LEG, NG3, LEAP, and others combine for 30+ Bcf/d capacity. Recent additions (LEG 1.8, NG3 2.2, LEAP 0.3 Bcf/d) serve LNG growth. Gulf South intrastate system estimated 10–25 miles from site.

15.3 Bcf/d LNG Capacity

Five operating facilities (Cameron, Corpus Christi, Sabine, Calcasieu Pass, Plaquemines) consume massive natural gas volumes. Plaquemines LNG (2.9 Bcf/d) located 50–75 miles south in same parish, with Gator Express feed pipeline.

Massive Materials Demand

LNG construction (1.5–2.5M CY concrete), data center buildout (Meta Richland Parish: 350K–500K CY), pipeline projects, and power generation drive 3–5 million CY concrete demand through 2030. Import gap creates terminal opportunity.

LNG Construction Demand Driver

LNG facilities under construction require 50,000–100,000 cubic yards concrete per 1.0 Bcf/d nameplate capacity. 12.9 Bcf/d under construction = 645,000–1,290,000 CY concrete demand. Combined with operating facility maintenance and data center/pipeline projects, total 2024–2030 demand reaches 3–5 million CY concrete, 5–10 million tons aggregates, 500K–1M tons cement.

Part 07

Regional Summary

Natural Gas Transmission

Southeast Louisiana hosts 10+ major interstate gas transmission systems with combined capacity exceeding 30 Bcf/d, serving LNG export facilities, industrial demand, and power generation across the Gulf Coast region.

LNG Export Infrastructure

Five operating Gulf Coast LNG facilities consume 15.3 Bcf/d of natural gas, with 12.9 Bcf/d of additional capacity under construction through 2028, requiring proportional pipeline infrastructure support.

Pipeline Proximity

Harvest CAM crude (<5 miles), Gulf South natural gas (10–25 miles), Cayenne NGL (10–20 miles), and Gator Express feed to Plaquemines LNG (50–75 miles) provide strong regional infrastructure context.

Regional Infrastructure Investment

$20–30 billion in pipeline, LNG, storage, and power generation projects are under construction or planned through 2030, representing sustained investment in the Southeast Louisiana energy corridor.
