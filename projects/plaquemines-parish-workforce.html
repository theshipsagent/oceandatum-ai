Part 01

Executive Summary

Project Overview

This project proposes development of industrial operations at a deep-water site in Braithwaite, Plaquemines Parish, Louisiana. This analysis evaluates the potential economic impact and workforce availability within the five-parish Greater New Orleans labor market area (Plaquemines, St. Bernard, Jefferson, Orleans, and St. Charles parishes).

The CCI Methanol Plant received full environmental and zoning approval for the same 387-acre site in 2020, demonstrating regulatory feasibility for heavy industrial development. This precedent is central to both the multiplier methodology and the regulatory pathway strategy.

Workforce Availability

  • 432,818 employed workers in five-parish labor shed

Key Findings

  1. Site Precedent Established: CCI Methanol Plant received full environmental and zoning approval for same 387-acre site in 2020, demonstrating regulatory feasibility for heavy industrial development.
  2. Robust Labor Market: Five-parish region provides sufficient workforce depth across all operational scenarios, with established industrial skills base in petrochemical, maritime, and manufacturing sectors.
  3. Competitive Wage Environment: Regional industrial wages range $72,000–$96,000 annually. Project wage structure must align with or exceed local market rates to attract qualified talent.
  4. Infrastructure Advantages: Port of Plaquemines offers 600+ miles of deep-draft (45+ ft) access, ranking among nation's top ports for bulk cargo handling.
  5. Workforce Development Resources: LED FastStart (ranked #1 nationally) provides customized training at no cost to employer for projects creating 15+ jobs.
  6. Multiplier Variance: LED precedents show employment multipliers ranging from 3.9x (Venture Global Plaquemines LNG) to 5.82x (CCI Methanol), creating 39% variance in total job projections. Use case determines appropriate multiplier selection.

Primary Recommendations

  1. Validation Pathway: For regulatory submissions, validate economic impact estimates through BEA RIMS II ($275–$500) or commission independent IMPLAN analysis ($3,000–$5,000).
  2. Regional Recruitment Strategy: Target St. Bernard and Jefferson parishes for skilled operations workforce; leverage existing industrial talent pool from petrochemical corridor. Structure three-tier recruitment:
    • Tier 1 (0–15 mi): Plaquemines + St. Bernard (28,758 workers)
    • Tier 2 (15–30 mi): Jefferson + Orleans (379,408 workers)
    • Tier 3 (30–40 mi): St. Charles (24,652 workers)
  3. Compensation Strategy: Establish competitive wage structure at $75,000–$95,000 range for skilled positions to compete effectively with energy sector employers including Venture Global LNG.
  4. Training Partnership: Engage LED FastStart early in project timeline to develop customized training curriculum aligned with operational requirements. Partner with Nunez Community College for long-term training pipeline.
  5. Regulatory Approach: Reference CCI Methanol Plant precedent in permitting discussions; same-site environmental review provides regulatory pathway template.
  6. Economic Impact Positioning: Use 3.9x multiplier (Venture Global) for regulatory submissions, 5.02x average for public presentations, and 3.9–5.82x range for investor materials with sensitivity analysis.
Part 02

Methodology & Validation Pathway

Working Model Disclosure

Current Status: This analysis employs a working economic impact model based on publicly available multiplier data from Louisiana Economic Development (LED). The methodology is consistent with Input-Output (I-O) economic modeling principles used by IMPLAN and BEA RIMS II platforms but does not constitute an official IMPLAN or RIMS II analysis.

The employment multiplier of 5.82 is derived from LED's economic impact analysis of the Castleton Commodities International (CCI) Methanol Plant project announced in 2014 for the same 387-acre site in Braithwaite, Plaquemines Parish, Louisiana. LED's analysis determined that 50 direct permanent jobs would generate 291 additional jobs in the regional economy (291 ÷ 50 = 5.82).

Annex B

Economic Multiplier Methodology

Type II Employment Multiplier

The 5.82 multiplier represents a Type II final-demand employment multiplier, meaning for every 1 direct job created, an additional 5.82 jobs are created through:

• Indirect effects (~2.9 jobs): Supply chain purchases from regional businesses (cement suppliers, equipment vendors, logistics providers, professional services)

• Induced effects (~2.9 jobs): Household spending by direct and indirect employees (retail, restaurants, housing, healthcare, personal services)

Calculation Formula: Total Employment Impact = Direct Jobs + (Direct Jobs × 5.82)

Example (Moderate Scenario): Direct Jobs: 125 → Indirect + Induced: 125 × 5.82 = 728 → Total: 125 + 728 = 853 jobs

Geographic Leakage

Not all economic activity stays in the local region. "Leakage" occurs when employees live outside the region and spend wages elsewhere, the facility purchases from suppliers outside the region, or workers save/invest income rather than spending locally.

Leakage Assumptions in This Analysis:

• Local Hiring: 70–80% of workers reside in 5-parish region

• Local Procurement: ~60% of B2B purchases from regional suppliers

• Local Spending: ~80% of household income spent in region

Limitations & Conservative Assumptions

  • Conservative Output Multiplier: Used 2.6x instead of higher estimates (2.8x+) for cement manufacturing
  • Moderate Wage Assumptions: $90,000 average operational wage is mid-range for industrial positions
  • Geographic Scope: Limited to 5-parish region; may understate broader regional impacts
  • Tax Revenue: Does not include property taxes (depends on ITEP exemption status)
  • Construction Phase: Treated as one-time impact, not annualized
