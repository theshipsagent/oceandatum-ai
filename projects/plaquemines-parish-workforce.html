Annex B: Economic Multiplier Methodology

Type II Employment Multiplier: Explanation

The 5.82 multiplier represents a Type II final-demand employment multiplier, meaning for every 1 direct job created, an additional 5.82 jobs are created through:

  • Indirect effects (~2.9 jobs): Supply chain purchases from regional businesses (cement suppliers, equipment vendors, logistics providers, professional services)
  • Induced effects (~2.9 jobs): Household spending by direct and indirect employees (retail, restaurants, housing, healthcare, personal services)

Calculation Methodology

Total Employment Impact = Direct Jobs + (Direct Jobs √ó 5.82)

Example (Moderate Scenario):

  • Direct Jobs: 125
  • Indirect + Induced Jobs: 125 √ó 5.82 = 728
  • Total Employment Impact: 125 + 728 = 853 jobs

How Multipliers Work: Visual Example

Project Masabba - Moderate Scenario Multiplier Effect

Step 1: Direct Impact

  • Facility hires 125 employees @ $90,000 average wage
  • Direct wages: $11.25 million annually
  • Facility generates $85 million in annual revenue

Step 2: Indirect Impact (Supply Chain)

  • Facility purchases $50 million in cement, equipment, logistics, services
  • Supplier companies hire workers to meet this demand
  • Indirect jobs created: 728 (5.82x multiplier)
  • Indirect wages: $6.75 million
  • Indirect output: $102 million

Step 3: Induced Impact (Household Spending)

  • 125 facility workers + 728 supplier workers = 853 workers earning wages
  • These 853 workers spend ~80% of wages locally ($14.4M spent)
  • Local businesses hire additional workers to meet this demand
  • Induced jobs created: 188
  • Induced wages: $3.38 million
  • Induced output: $34 million

Total Economic Impact:

  • Total Jobs: 1,040 (125 + 728 + 188) [Conservative: 853]
  • Total Wages: $21.38 million
  • Total Output: $221 million

Geographic Leakage

Not all economic activity stays in the local region. "Leakage" occurs when:

  • Employees live outside the region and spend wages elsewhere
  • Facility purchases from suppliers outside the region
  • Workers save/invest income rather than spending locally

Leakage Assumptions in This Analysis:

  • Local Hiring: 70-80% of workers reside in 5-parish region
  • Local Procurement: ~60% of B2B purchases from regional suppliers
  • Local Spending: ~80% of household income spent in region

Limitations and Conservative Assumptions

  • Conservative Output Multiplier: Used 2.6x instead of higher estimates (2.8x+) for cement manufacturing
  • Moderate Wage Assumptions: $90,000 average operational wage is mid-range for industrial positions
  • Geographic Scope: Limited to 5-parish region, may understate broader regional impacts
  • Tax Revenue: Does not include property taxes (depends on ITEP exemption status)
  • Construction Phase: Treated as one-time impact, not annualized

These conservative assumptions mean actual economic impacts may exceed projections, providing a margin of safety in presentations to investors and economic development officials.
