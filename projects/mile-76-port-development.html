Project Overview: This engagement provides the full administrative and research infrastructure for a port terminal acquisition at a 387-acre deep-water site on the Lower Mississippi River. Work spans navigation and hydrographic assessment, regulatory and environmental compliance review, infrastructure condition analysis, commodity market evaluation, workforce economics, and financial modeling — delivering a comprehensive decision-support package for site acquisition and development planning.

Project-Specific Distinguishing Factors

CCI Methanol Precedent — Same Site

Louisiana Economic Development previously analyzed this exact 387-acre Braithwaite location for the CCI Methanol Plant (2014). The state-verified 5.82x employment multiplier provides a defensible, site-specific baseline unavailable for most greenfield analyses.

World-Class Deep-Draft Access

45-foot maintained channel depth with 100+ miles of continuous deep-draft access to the Gulf. Site ranks in the top tier of US port locations for bulk and liquid bulk vessel compatibility — a rare combination with proximity to petrochemical and industrial demand centers.

Multi-Scenario Commodity Modeling

Three development scenarios fully modeled — methanol export, ammonia export, and bulk industrial minerals — each with independent economic impact, construction phase, and workforce projections, allowing comparative analysis for capital allocation decisions.

USACE Permit Corridor Database

Custom-built permit database covering 66 authorizations along Mile 25–235 of the Lower Mississippi River, with 11 full permit PDFs downloaded and indexed. This live permit intelligence is not available from any public source in compiled form.

LED Incentive Stack Analysis

Full qualification assessment for Louisiana's industrial incentive stack: LED FastStart (free workforce training), Industrial Tax Exemption Program (ITEP), Quality Jobs Program, and Tax Increment Financing — quantified and integrated into the financial model.

Five-Parish Labor Shed Mapping

Granular workforce availability mapped across Plaquemines, St. Bernard, Jefferson, Orleans, and St. Charles parishes — 432,818 employed workers — with wage benchmarking against active competitors including Baker Hughes, Chevron Oronite, and Venture Global.
