Critical Design Considerations

  • Revetment Clearance: Buoys positioned in main channel to avoid interference with bank protection systems
  • River Flow Dynamics: Positioned to minimize current stress while providing optimal vessel approach angles
  • Navigation Safety: Coordinated with USCG for proper marking, lighting, and chart notation
  • Seasonal Adjustment: Chain scope allows for 40+ foot river stage variations without repositioning

Permit Overview

Pelican Terminals LLC (Applicant: Juan Carlos Carbone) filed USACE Public Notice MVN-2025-00276-EPP for a proposed midstream dry-cargo mooring facility at Bohemia, Louisiana — Mississippi River Mile 46.3 Above Head of Passes (AHP). Engineering drawings prepared by Lanier & Associates, licensed engineers. The 20-sheet permit set includes vicinity maps, general notes, existing and proposed site plans, and detailed cross-section profiles.

Proposed Facility Configuration: A 7-tier × 8-wide barge fleet (56 barges) anchored by 12 typical monopiles, a captive barge dock, a 250′ × 72′ crane barge, 200′ × 35′ product barges, and a midstream ship berth (750′ × 106′) served by five anchor piles and five navigation buoys. Total facility designed for Panamax+ class bulk carriers.

Key Elevations: Project flowline +11.25′ NAVD 88  |  Low Water Reference Plane (LWRP) +0.25′  |  Anchor pile tip elevation −200.0′ NAVD 88  |  Coordinates: 29.551389°N, 89.764444°W
