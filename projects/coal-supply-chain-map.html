US Coal Supply Chain Map

Production basins, mine sites, rail corridors, barge waterways, and export terminals — interactive geospatial reference

Geospatial Coal Supply Chain v2 — 2026-03-02 MSHA | BTS NTAD | Class I Rail
An interactive supply chain reference map covering the full US coal production and export geography. Seven major basins from the Powder River Basin to the Gulf Lignite fields, 77 major mine sites sized by annual output, Class I rail corridors, 373 segments of navigable coal waterways, and the five principal US export terminals. Built from MSHA mine registry data aggregated through 2024, BTS NTAD waterway networks, and field-verified terminal specs.

Coal Basins

7 basins: Powder River (PRB), Central Appalachia (CAPP), Northern Appalachia (NAPP), Illinois Basin (ILB), Black Warrior, Uinta-Piceance (UIB), Gulf Lignite

Mine Sites

77 major mines (≥1M ST/yr, always visible) + 152 smaller operations (clustered layer, toggle on). Circle size = production. Color = coal type.

Class I Rail

BNSF, UP, CSX, NS, CN, CPRS — the six principal carriers moving coal from basin to port. Color-coded by railroad.

Waterways

373 navigable segments: Ohio River system, Lower Mississippi, Monongahela, Black Warrior-Tombigbee, and Illinois River. BTS NTAD source.

Export Terminals

5 US export terminals with annual capacity, max vessel draft, and rail connection data. Tooltip on click.

Coal Type Colors

Deep red = HCC (coking) · Red = Met · Amber = Sub-bit (PRB) · Purple = ILB thermal · Blue = thermal bituminous · Gray = lignite
