Historical Background

The Port Sulphur site operated as a major sulfur processing terminal from 1933 to 1978, utilizing the Frasch Process for sulfur extraction and handling. The facility was constructed by Freeport Sulphur Company and represented a significant industrial operation in Plaquemines Parish for over 70 years.

Key Historical Milestones

  • 1933: Facility construction and operations commenced
  • 1933-1978: Active sulfur processing using Frasch Process
  • 2005: Hurricane Katrina caused 22-foot storm surge and facility damage
  • 2009: Emergency response for 5.8 million pounds acidic water release
  • Present: Site marketed for redevelopment as marine terminal

Chapter 4: Environmental Due Diligence

Historical Operations Summary

The Port Sulphur facility operated as a major sulfur extraction and processing terminal from 1933 to 2005, representing 72 years of industrial activity. The Frasch Process, used extensively at this site, involved injecting superheated water (300°F+) into underground sulfur deposits, melting the sulfur, and pumping it to the surface for processing and barge loading.

Contamination Concerns

Soil Contamination

  • Acidification: Sulfur oxidation creates sulfuric acid; soil pH <3.0 in some areas
  • Residual Sulfur: Up to 50,900 mg/kg in former vatting areas (EPA industrial soil screening level: 3,900 mg/kg)
  • Heavy Metals: Acid mobilization of iron, aluminum, zinc, lead, arsenic from natural sediments
  • Petroleum Hydrocarbons: Potential contamination from equipment operations, hydraulic fluids

Groundwater Concerns

  • Acidic Plume: Historical low-pH groundwater from sulfuric acid generation
  • Dissolved Metals: Iron, aluminum, manganese mobilized in acidic conditions
  • Sulfate Concentrations: Elevated sulfate from sulfur oxidation processes

Structural/Infrastructure Contamination

  • Creosote-Treated Timber: Dock pilings, bulkhead likely treated with creosote (PAH contamination)
  • Lead-Based Paint: Historical structures likely contain lead paint
  • Asbestos: Building materials in warehouse/office structures
