The Problem This Solves

Hold cleaning is one of the most underestimated cost items in a vessel port call. A quick Proforma Disbursement Account (PDA) might note it as a round number — but the actual cost varies by a factor of five depending on what the ship previously carried.

A Supramax that just discharged nitrogen fertilizer needs a different cleaning protocol than one that discharged coal — and the difference can mean two extra days at anchor, a failed USDA inspection, and a $40,000 bill instead of $10,000.

This module makes that calculation explicit, defensible, and fast — combining cargo compatibility intelligence, regulatory requirements, and real market rate data in a single lookup.

The hidden cost: Every US grain export requires a mandatory USDA FGIS Stowage Examination and NCB Certificate of Readiness — regardless of how clean the holds are. That inspection fee is $3,000–$4,500 before a single mop is lifted.
A single missed protocol: Using the bilge pump after a cement cargo destroys the pump. Washing copper concentrate with water creates a concrete-like layer requiring abrasive disc grinding. These are career-ending mistakes — and both are in the module's warning system.

The Five Cleanliness Standards

The required standard is set by the cargo being loaded — not what was previously carried. The previous cargo determines how difficult it is to achieve that standard.

RankStandardRequired ForSurveyor Tolerance
1 Hospital Clean Soda ash, kaolin, mineral sands, rice, titanium dioxide, food-grade cargo Zero tolerance — no dust, stain, odor, or loose scale. Shore gang almost always required.
2 Grain Clean All grains (corn, soybeans, wheat, sorghum), alumina, sulphur, cement, fertilizers No loose residue, no odor, bilge wells clean and dry. FGIS/NCB inspection mandatory for US exports.
3 Normal Clean General bulk cargo where CP does not specify higher standard Previous cargo residues removed. No significant contamination risk.
4 Shovel Clean Coal, iron ore, ores in repeat COA trades Bulk of cargo removed. Some residue acceptable. Not suitable before grain or white cargo.
5 Load on Top Same commodity COA trade only New cargo loaded over residue of previous. Never permitted for grain.

Regulatory Framework Covered

USDA FGIS / NCB — Mandatory for US Grain Exports

  • USDA Federal Grain Inspection Service Stowage Examination (7 CFR Part 800)
  • NCB Certificate of Readiness (stability, bilges, structure)
  • Per-hold cleanliness certificates ($260/hold)
  • No US grain terminal will load without a valid FGIS certificate
  • Failure = vessel detained at anchor. Off-hire risk under charter party.

MARPOL Annex V — Wash Water Discharge

  • HME cargoes (coal, petcoke, fertilizers): discharge PROHIBITED at sea
  • Port reception facility mandatory for all HME wash water
  • Chemical wash water always treated as HME regardless of cargo type
  • Gulf of Mexico = MARPOL Special Area (stricter rules)
  • Garbage Record Book entries required for all cargo residue discharge
